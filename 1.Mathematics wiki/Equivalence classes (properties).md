> [!quote] Theorem: Equivalence classes (properties)
> Let $\sim$ be a equivalence relation on some set $X$, then for all $x,y\in X$ 
> 1. $$ x\in [x] $$
> 2. $$ x \sim y \implies [x]=[y] $$
> 3. $$ \neg (x \sim y) \implies [x]\cap[y]=\emptyset$$
