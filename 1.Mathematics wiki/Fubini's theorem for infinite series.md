> [!quote] Theorem: Fubini's theorem for infinite series
> Let $f:\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}\to \mathbb{R}$ be a function such that $\sum^{}_{(n,m)\in \mathbb{N}\times \mathbb{N}}f(n,m)$ is absolutely convergent. Then $$\begin{align}\sum^{\infty}_{n=0} \left( \sum^{\infty}_{m=0} f(n,m) \right)&= \sum^{}_{(n,m)\in \mathbb{N}\times \mathbb{N}}f(n,m) \\   &=\sum^{}_{(m,n)\in \mathbb{N}\times \mathbb{N}}f(n,m) \\ &=\sum^{\infty}_{m=0} \left( \sum^{\infty}_{n=0} f(n,m) \right)  \end{align}$$
